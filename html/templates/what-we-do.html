What We Do

Our Goals

Open data, like any public resource, needs to be developed, managed, and provided in a way that meets the needs of the people and organizations that use it. However, there are currently few effective ways for data users to give input and feedback that can improve the open data ecosystem. We seek to fill this gap through a better understanding of open data users and greater engagement of stakeholders. Working with both data providers and users, we develop strategies for smarter open data investments and improvements.
Our goal is to facilitate: 

  • Better informed policy-making
  • More targeted solutions
  • A higher ROI for open data

We Map

The Open Data Enterprise Map will curate and display open data use cases around the world. It will include a searchable, sortable database of use cases to help stakeholders:

  • Analyze key trends and best practices in the use of open data
  • Identify high value datasets and specific areas for improvement 
  • Develop the rationale for worldwide government investment in open data resources

The Enterprise Map will build directly on our team’s experience developing the Open Data 500 study at The GovLab, the first comprehensive study of US companies using open data to develop new business, products, and services. Since the launch of the Open Data 500 in spring 2014, we have worked with the governments of Australia and Mexico on similar studies.

We Convene

“The U.S. Open Data Action Plan, released by the White House in May 2014, describes our Roundtables as key to the government’s commitment to “support innovators and improve open data based on feedback.”

We host and facilitate structured dialogues that bring open data users and providers together to enable feedback, brainstorming, collaboration and planning to improve the open data ecosystem.

Through the Open Data Roundtables, we identify high-value datasets, find solutions to data problems, and establish new collaborations between open data users and providers.

The Roundtables are designed to:
  • Prioritize the most important datasets in each agency for business and public use
  • Improve each agency’s data and make it easier to find, access, and work with
  • Connect users with government agency staff who manage the data they use, and set up a process for ongoing feedback.

  • Department of Commerce: Working with “America’s data agency” on the potential of open data from the Census Bureau, National Atmospheric and Oceanic Administration, the Patent and Trademark Office, and Bureau of Economic Analysis.

We Implement

The Open Data Roundtables have identified a wide range of opportunities for the public, private, and nonprofit sectors to find new solutions to problems in open data’s management and use. The Center works with all stakeholders to find practical solutions that can help achieve common goals such as:

  • Making it easier to discover and find data and access data
  • Improving data quality - making it more complete, valid, and accurate
  • Making data interoperable (that is, easier to combine and compare with other data)
