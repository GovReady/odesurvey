Demand-Driven Open Data

Bringing users and providers together for smarter data strategies.

The Center for Open Data Enterprise 

is an independent non-profit organization based in Washington, DC. 
We focus on the demand side of open data for better informed policy making, more targeted solutions, and to maximize return on investments in open data.

What is Open Data?

Open data is free, publicly available data that anyone can use and access, without restrictions. Open data can be used by businesses nonprofits, governments and citizens to launch new initiatives and ventures, analyze trends, make data-driven decisions, and solve complex problems. 

“We recognize the urgent need to get this right, and we know that only by listening to the business community, partnering with industry, and collaborating with fellow government agencies, can we best serve our customers and unleash the full power and potential of open data.”

- Bruce Andrews, Deputy Secretary, U.S. Department of Commerce
