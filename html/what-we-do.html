What We Do

Our user-centered approach aims to improve the open data ecosystem in three ways.
We MAP the uses of open data from around the world; CONVENE data users and providers to identify challenges and opportunities; and IMPLEMENT solutions driven by user input.

WE MAP.

The open data impact map is the first centralized, searchable database of open data use cases around the world, including an interactive visualization. The map will be a key resource in understanding the range of applications and types of open data innovation, and demonstrate the value of open government data around the world. Read more.

WE CONVENE.

We bring together open data providers and users for structured dialogues to help make government open data more relevant, accessible, and actionable. Through the Open Data Roundtables, originally launched as an initiative of The GovLab at NYU, we help identify high-value datasets, find solutions to data problems, and establish new public-private collaborations. Read more. 

WE IMPLEMENT.

The Open Data Roundtables have identified a wide range of opportunities for the public, private, and nonprofit sectors to find new solutions to problems in open data’s management and use. Read more.
